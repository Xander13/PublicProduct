Google Braille Pixel Slate


Motion Grapics

Role: Motion Graphics

Contribution: Alex Kauffman & Nik Delgado

Duration: 4 weeks

Target User: Blind User

Google Creative Lab & New Media Design

We had an RIT Alumni from Google creative lab and he and my professor gave us a random proposal generator to design from. Our design challenge was Disability + Calendar + Tactile.

Our idea was taking ideas that aren't too far outside of technology advance and improve many millions of blind users. We wanted to improve the cost and the functionality of the braille machine.

Branding

As we did this project, we made sure we follow the google branding guidelines. With the following branding guidelines, it helps us know our boundaries and improve visual graphics more like google styles.

Google visual guidelines checklist


Clairty

We wanted to show right up front what the Google braille pixel slate is all about. We followed the branding guidelines about not adding extra elements in the background or anything other than the product itself.

Inclusiveness

As the Google marketing guidelines, we made sure we checked off the "Are we being inclusive?" because we want to show how google has emerging technology that can help a large group of blind users.

Up to date

At first, we did the google pixel hub and pain points were using that product but we also realized the pixel slate is a new family that has a lot more potential for blind users. The pixel slate is a much easier technology to interact with for blind users.

Style frames

Pain Points



Costly

Today braille machines are very expensive. They can cost around 4 thousand dollars per machines.

Outdated

Most braille machines only interacts with one line of text and they are big clunky machines. They are not visual aesthetically pleasing from non blind users.

Limited app integrations

Only can read or write in the braille machines or readers.

Our challenges

Our first idea was taking an existing object google had and make it accessible for a blind user. We realized that the google home hub would be painful to use due to the angle of the tablet is on. Their hand would be in pain for a long while of the interaction of this product. We scratched that and went back to the drawing board made sure we make it easier for them to use.

We did not forget!

We are currently creating on the voice over for this project to make it accessible for blind users to know about this project.
